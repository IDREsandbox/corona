To better understand and visualize the global and local spread of the COVID-19 epidemic, a group of researchers at the UCLA Institute for Digital Research and Education have developed this map-based data dashboard that provides an interactive timeline from January 22 to present day.

This project was inspired by 2019 Novel Coronavirus COVID-19 (2019-nCoV) Data Repository by Johns Hopkins CSSE from which global data is derived from. Local data for California and Los Angeles comes from the Los Angeles Times coronavirus tracking page.

The main feature of this visualization is in its ability to “scrub” through time. Use your left ← and right → arrow keys to navigate back and forth in one day increments. You can also drag the red circle and slide it across the time line, or click on an area on the red line to jump to a specific day.

Circle colors are represented by growth rate, calculated as a percent change from the previous day. If the growth is greater than 50% from the day before, it is represented by a red circle; no change is represented by a semi-transparent white circle.
